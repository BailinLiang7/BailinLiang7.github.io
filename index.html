📝Publications & Patents

Generalized Fault-Tolerant Control for Multi-Wheel Corner-Module Vehicles via Reconfigurable MPC

Bailin Liang, Junnian Wang*, Zidong Zhou, Guoshun Cai, and Yong Wang*

IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON INDUSTRIAL INFORMATICS (JCR Q1; IF=9.9), submitted.

This paper proposes a Generalized Fault-Tolerant Control (GFT-RMPC) framework for multi-wheel corner-module vehicles, enabling cross-platform adaptation to arbitrary chassis configurations (4W/6W and beyond). It combines automatic topology identification, actuator-health-aware MPC reconfiguration, and physical latent space optimization to maintain stability and tracking accuracy across multi-scenario failures. Hardware-in-the-Loop testing verifies real-time execution within 12.36ms, confirming the practical deployability and robustness.

Nonlinear Shimmy Analysis of Highly Integrated In-Wheel Motor Driving Unit with Independent Kingpin Steering for Electric Vehicles

Bailin Liang, Junnian Wang*, Yitong Wang, Chunlin Zhang, Yinpu Qi, and Heping Ling

Vehicle System Dynamics (JCR Q1; IF=3.9), submitted.

This work Developed a nonlinear shimmy dynamics model for highly integrated in-wheel driving units (HIDUs), capturing wheel-alignment effects and suspension–wheel coupling dynamics. Proposed a Fast Parametric Sensitivity Analysis (FPSA) framework by integrating CX-A and Energy Flow (EF) methods, significantly improving analysis efficiency compared with conventional simulation-based approaches. Revealed self-excited shimmy behavior of HIDS and the energy-transfer mechanisms between wheel shimmy and suspension roll motion, providing theoretical guidance for chassis design optimization and control strategy development.

🔬Current Research

Dynamics-Aware VLM-MPC Framework for Autonomous Vehicles

Current VLM-based planning methods often overlook differences in vehicle dynamic capabilities across chassis configurations and driving scenarios, which can lead to unreliable or dynamically infeasible trajectories. This work proposes a dynamics-aware VLM planning and MPC tracking framework that integrates real-time vehicle motion envelopes with closed-loop trajectory feasibility feedback, enabling the VLM-MPC architecture to better adapt to diverse vehicle configurations while improving planning reliability and safety.

Shimmy Stability Control of Highly Integrated In-Wheel Motor Driving Units

This work focuses on shimmy stability control for Highly Integrated In-Wheel Motor Driving Units (HIDUs), a by-wire modular chassis unit integrating driving, braking, steering, and suspension functions. It aims to analyze HIDU shimmy mechanisms and develop a coordinated driving-braking-steering control strategy for active shimmy suppression.
